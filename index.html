How is Christmas Celebrated in South Korea?

Christmas in South Korea is celebrated in a unique and modern way, blending Western influences with Korean culture.
Here's a quick overview of how it's typically observed

Christmas in South Korea is a unique blend of Western tradition and Korean culture.
As the only East Asian country that recognizes Christmas (Sung Tan Jul) as a national holiday,
South Korea embraces the festive spirit with enthusiasm. Unlike the family-centered celebrations common in the West,
Christmas in Korea is often seen as a romantic occasion,similar to Valentine’s Day,where couples go on dates,exchange gifts,
and enjoy special dinners.
Cities like Seoul transform into dazzling winter wonderlands, especially in shopping districts such as Myeongdong and Gangnam,
with Christmas trees, LED displays, and themed pop-up events.The holiday is highly commercialized,with stores offering major sales
and restaurants crafting seasonal menus, while the religious aspect is less prominent for most.
However, for the Christian minority—around 30% of the population—church services and carol singing remain meaningful traditions during this time.

Christmas Food in South Korea

If people do opt to celebrate with family, many will share a large meal with plenty of delicious side dishes.
Instead of a traditional Western Christmas roast, you might find the table adorned with South Korean classics like sweet potato noodles
and the ever-present kimchi.
